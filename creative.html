Colour the spring

Pick a crayon, click any part of the picture, and bring the weight on a spring to life in your own colours. When you are happy with it, download your version for the report or to print.

Crayons

Why a colouring page?

Slowing down to colour something in is a surprisingly good way to understand it. Filling in the spring, the shock absorber and each fading wave is a quiet way to read the picture one part at a time.

It also doubles as the creative side of our project: a single image that carries the whole story, a weight, a spring that pulls it back, something slowing it down, and a motion that gently fades.

Make it yours

Colour it on screen and download a crisp copy, or clear everything first and print the blank picture as a hand out. Matching the colours to the graphs elsewhere on the site ties the whole project together.
